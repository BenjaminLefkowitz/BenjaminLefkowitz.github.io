Twenty Years of ticket sales for the three Metro Based NHL Teams

Looking at Arenas and Cities by comparing the Rangers, Devils, and Islanders

Benjamin Lefkowitz

Photo taken by Benjamin Lefkowitz

The three New York area times: NY Rangers, NY Islanders, and NJ Devils have storied histories with both major triumphs and team disappointments. The venues for all three teams have gone through renovations and changes as well. For this project I looked at the attendance of all three teams over the past thirty years. Taking into account several changes for the arenas including the Devils move from the Meadowlands to the Prudential Center in the 2007-08 season, the switch for the Islanders from the Nassau Coliseum to Barclays Center, and then to their current home at UBS Arena, and the renovations that occurred at Madison Square Garden. Throughout all of these changes, attendance for games never faltered, but one team stood out above the rest.

The Rangers, an iconic original six team in the NHL, who plays at the world most famous arena, Madison Square Garden, had the highest attendance based on area capacity out of all the teams. Throughout the twenty years, the data showed the Rangers having a consistently higher attendance than both the Islanders and Devils. In the first figure displaying season attendance as a percentage of the area capacity the Rangers never faltered under 94 percent of Madison Square Garden being filled. While the team had on-ice success what makes going to Ranger games extremely appealing is the reputation and experience of Madison Square Garden. .
